I'm Angus.

But you can call me Mango. Welcome to my personal site – a bit 2003, but it turns out that's all you need.

Recently moved to Vancouver after 6 years in London. It's wet (famously), but it's an amazing city. It keeps me active – follow me on Strava to see which trails I've been visiting recently!

I'm currently working for Mastercard in a strategy & operations role. Often working on our enterprise strategy, sometimes acquisition opportunities, and with a few shots of product management and marketing for good measure. I recently started up a Tech Leadership Network for our 250-person Vancouver Tech Hub – I'd love to hear from you if you're local.

For 5 years I sat on the Board of Directors for Data Driven Charities, a data-for-good philanthropic organisation. I reviewed dozens of project proposals, and together we distributed $200K to non-profits looking to improve their impact through the use of data. The best part was the follow-up – hearing about our grant recipients' project progress and measurable impact.

Also, talk to me about maths – I spent 4 years studying it (mostly applied). Statistics for networks, fluid dynamics, statistical mechanics, Black-Scholes, stochastics for biology, and a bit of machine learning. The IMA published a paper I authored on the origins of group theory. My one wish: that I'd completed a joint honours with computer science or philosophy.
